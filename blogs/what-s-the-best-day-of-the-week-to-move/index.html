You can’t stop traffic or predict detours, but you can choose the right day to hit the road and move smarter.

Most people focus on packing, planning, and hiring movers, but they often overlook one crucial question that can make a significant difference: what’s the best day of the week to move?

So, the short answer is Tuesday and Wednesday. These midweek movers are often the most affordable, least stressful, and easiest to book.

But they’re not your only options. 

If you're flexible, a few other days can work surprisingly well as long as you avoid the most popular (and problematic) ones.

In fact, a recent study shows that moving on a weekday can save you 30–60% compared to moving on a weekend.  

That’s a major difference from just shifting your schedule by a day or two. 

So, before you circle a date on the calendar, let’s discuss the ideal days to move and why timing matters more than you think.

Why the Day of the Week Matters for Moving

Choosing the best day of the week to move isn’t just about selecting a time that fits your schedule; it’s also about saving money, minimizing stress, and making the entire process smoother.

Cost Differences: Some days experience higher demand, which can lead to increased prices. Moving midweek, like on Tuesday or Wednesday, often means lower rates and less competition for services.

Mover Availability: Movers are easier to book on less popular days. Choosing a day with lighter demand gives you better flexibility and improves your chances of getting your preferred time.

Traffic and Timing: Midweek moves tend to be less affected by delays. Lighter neighborhood traffic and a calmer pace help everything stay on track.

Ideal Days for a Smooth Relocation

If you're looking for a stress-free, cost-effective move, these days consistently deliver better results than others.

Tuesday: This day is often considered the hidden gem of moving. Demand is low, availability is high, and prices tend to be more affordable. Plus, there's usually less road traffic, especially in residential areas.

Wednesday: Close behind Tuesday, Wednesday offers many of the same benefits. It's far enough from the weekend rush, making it easier to schedule movers, avoid delays, and keep things calm and manageable.

Decent Alternatives for Flexible Movers

While Tuesday and Wednesday are top-tier choices, not everyone can make a midweek move work. If your schedule is tight but you still want to avoid peak chaos, these days are your next best options.

Thursday: This day offers many midweek benefits while giving you a head start on settling in before the weekend. It’s a great choice if you want to unpack and get organized without taking too much time off work.

Monday: While it sits right after the weekend, Monday can be a good option if you book early. Mover availability tends to improve, and traffic remains lighter than it will be later in the week.

Days to Avoid for Stress-Free Moving

While some days make your move easier, others can have the opposite effect, bringing higher costs, tighter schedules, and added stress.

If you’re aiming for a smooth move, it’s just as important to know which days to avoid as it is to know the best ones. After all, even good planning can be disrupted by poor timing.

Friday: Many people try to move before the weekend, which makes Friday one of the busiest days for moving companies. High demand often leads to higher prices and limited availability.

Saturday: Although it may seem convenient, Saturday moves often come with heavy traffic, limited booking options, and higher rates. Movers are often stretched thin, which can impact service quality.

Sunday: Although it may be a day off for you, it’s a slower and more limited day for many moving companies. Some even close or offer reduced hours, making it harder to get help if issues come up.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Moving Day

By now, you know which days are ideal and which ones are to avoid. But sometimes, life doesn't let you choose freely. Work schedules, school calendars, and other responsibilities can limit your options.

That’s why it helps to weigh the bigger picture when picking your date.

Cost and Budget: If saving money is a priority, aim for midweek. Rates are usually lower when demand is down. Even shifting your move by a day or two can make a noticeable difference.

Availability and Convenience: Some days book up fast, especially during peak seasons. The more flexible you are, the more likely you'll get your ideal time and crew.

Traffic and Logistics: Residential areas tend to be less congested midweek. Fewer cars and clearer access points can keep the process running smoothly and on time.

Work and School Schedule: For families or working individuals, choosing a day that causes minimal disruption to routine life creates a more stable and manageable transition.

Weather and Seasonal Considerations: Summer is the most popular time to move, but it’s also the busiest and hottest. If you can, plan around extreme weather or peak seasons to avoid unnecessary complications.

Smart Tips for Choosing the Best Moving Day

Once you’ve considered your options, it’s time to finalize the day that works best for you. If you’re aiming for midweek savings or working around a busy schedule, these simple tips can help you plan with confidence.

Tip

Why It Matters

Book Early

Securing your date in advance offers better pricing, increased availability, and less stress.

Move in the Morning

Early starts allow for more time to accommodate unexpected delays and ensure smoother overall progress.

Avoid Rush Hour

Moving outside peak traffic times helps ensure easier loading and less stress.

Have a Backup Plan

Unexpected issues happen, and flexibility helps you stay on track and avoid panic.

Check Building Rules

Some buildings restrict moving times; check ahead to avoid surprises on move day.

Whatâ€™s the best time of the month to move?

Mid-month is usually the sweet spot. The beginning and end of each month are peak periods due to lease turnovers, which often lead to higher rates and tighter schedules. Moving mid-month often means lower demand and better flexibility.

Does the time of day affect moving costs?

In some cases, yes. Many moving companies prefer early morning moves, and booking one can sometimes save you money. Morning moves also help you avoid afternoon traffic, giving you a full day to settle in and unpack.

Can I move during a holiday week?

You can, but it comes with challenges. Movers may have limited staff, and holiday surcharges might apply. Traffic and parking can also be unpredictable. If youâ€™re considering a holiday move, book well in advance and double-check availability.

Do moving companies work seven days a week?

Some do, but not all. Sunday service is often limited or unavailable, especially in smaller markets. Always confirm hours and availability early, especially if you're aiming for a less conventional move date, such as a Sunday or a holiday.

Should I take time off work to move?

Yes, taking at least one full day off is highly recommended. Moving can be unpredictable, and trying to juggle work responsibilities adds unnecessary stress.
