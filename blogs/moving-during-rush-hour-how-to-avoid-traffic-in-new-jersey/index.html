Moving During Rush Hour – How to Avoid Traffic in New Jersey

Home / Blogs / Moving During Rush Hour – How to Avoid Traffic in New Jersey

Ezra M.

Published Date: 25 Jul 2025

Share:

Timing isn’t everything; it’s the only thing when it comes to moving. If there’s ever a time when that quote hits home, it’s when you’re moving during rush hour in New Jersey.

This isn’t just about loading boxes into a truck and hitting the road. It’s about when you hit the road. And if you get the timing wrong? You could turn what should be a 30-minute move into a three-hour traffic nightmare.

New Jersey isn’t just crowded; it’s the most densely populated state in the U.S., with over 9.2 million people crammed into a tight network of highways, bridges, and backroads. During peak commute times, typically 6:30 to 9:30 am and 3:30 to 6:30 pm, those roads become a slow-moving maze.

That’s why you need to know how to avoid the worst traffic in New Jersey. Let's discuss real strategies, expert insights, local traffic patterns, and smart planning tools to help you execute a smooth move, even if you're relocating during rush hour.

If you’re asking yourself, “Is it bad to move during rush hour?”—the short answer is yes. However, with the right planning and timing, it doesn't have to be that way.

Why Timing Matters: The New Jersey Rush Hour Reality

First off, New Jersey is one of the most densely populated states in the US—millions of cars, narrow roads, and jammed highways during peak commute hours.

Rush hour typically starts early, from 6:00 am to 9:30 am in the morning and from 3:30 pm to 6:30 pm in the afternoon.

During those windows, hundreds of thousands of commuters flood major corridors like I-78, I-287, the Turnpike, and the Garden State Parkway. Drivers familiar with this know: if you hit the road at the wrong time, even a short move can take ages.

Morning Rush Hour in NJ

The busiest half hour nationally is between 7:00 and 7:29 am, with over 14% of commuters on the road then. Following that, significant traffic remains until about 9 am. This trend is especially true in the NYC-adjacent New Jersey suburbs.

So if you’re moving during rush hour and you pull out your moving truck at 7:15 am, it’s going to be crawling.

Afternoon Rush Hour in NJ

Evening traffic picks up around 3:30 pm, peaking between 5:00 pm and 6:00 pm. The congestion can be even worse than in the mornings, with double the risk of accidents and major slowdowns.

How to Avoid Rush Hour When Moving in New Jersey

Let's break this down step-by-step so you can avoid rush hour when moving:

Determine Your Route & Local Peaks

If you're moving from Newark through the Turnpike or I-78 toward Jersey City or southern NJ, avoid leaving between 6–9 am and 3:30–6:30 pm. That’s classic peak commute traffic.

According to NJDOT data, congestion duration and travel times during peak hours have steadily increased over the years, so it pays to plan smart.

 

Tip: Use real-time traffic apps and local traffic reports, such as NJDOT transit alerts, to spot accidents or bottlenecks and select alternate routes.

Choose the Best Time to Move During Rush Hour

This could sound funny, but the best time to move during rush hour is actually not during rush hour. Here’s what experts say:

  • The ideal window is early morning between 7:00 am and 10:00 am, traffic is lighter, temperatures are cooler, and you get most of the day.
  • If you can’t do that, after 9:30 am is an excellent alternative—roads start clearing around 8:30 to 9, and by 10, traffic is typically manageable.
  • Avoid mid-afternoon into early evening—that’s when you’re right in the thick of evening rush.

Plan Around Peak Commute Times

Is it bad to move during rush hour? The answer is: Yes—it’s one of the worst times. Traffic is stop-and-go, moving trucks struggle in tight lanes, and stress levels skyrocket.

If you're forced into that window:

  • Use rush hour moving day strategies. Plan a route with fewer highway miles, schedule extra buffer time, and coordinate with your movers to start loading before peak hours begin.
  • Consider moving at peak commute times only if absolutely needed. If your building requires an afternoon elevator reservation, compensate with an even earlier departure.

Safety & Truck Considerations

While planning, factor in moving truck rush hour tips:

  • Trucks accelerate slowly and have trouble weaving in gridlock. That means if you're bumper to bumper, progress is halted.
  • Rush hour move safety tips: maintain a safe distance, avoid lane changes, and watch out for aggressive drivers. In a rush, accidents spike in NJ, particularly in evening commutes.
  • Avoid distractions: keep calls minimal, stay calm, and remind your movers to maintain clear communication.

How to Plan a Move During Peak Hours (When You Must)

Maybe your timeline is tight, or your building’s moving policy locks you into a certain window. Here's how to plan a move during peak hours:

  1. Start loading early and get everything packed and on-site before the peak begins.
  2. Book your moving truck departure to occur 15–30 minutes before the worst of the commute. That way, you're on the road while traffic is still building slowly.
  3. Use real-time traffic updates and avoid known bottlenecks. NJDOT and navigation apps show live changes.
  4. Have alternative routes ready—local roads might take longer in terms of distance, but could be faster in terms of time.
  5. Keep buffer time, expect delays, so don’t schedule anything tight immediately after arrival.

Traffic Tips for Moving Day

Moving during rush hour in New Jersey isn’t just about hitting the road; it’s about doing it with a plan. To help you stay ahead of traffic and avoid frustration, here are some key traffic tips for moving day.

Check the Weather Before You Leave

Always check the forecast ahead of time. Rain, snow, or even heavy fog can quickly turn a smooth move into a slippery mess. New Jersey weather can shift fast, especially in the fall and winter months.

Don’t Overload Your Moving Truck

A common mistake is trying to fit everything into one trip. However, an overloaded truck can be difficult to maneuver, slow to accelerate, and more challenging to stop, especially in traffic. Keeping the load manageable is safer and smarter, especially if you're moving at peak commute times.

Pick a Lane and Stick with It

Changing lanes constantly in heavy traffic doesn’t save time. It usually adds stress. Choose the most appropriate lane for your route—typically the middle lane on highways—and stay there unless you absolutely need to exit.

Build in Extra Travel Time

Even if your move is only 10 miles away, double—or even triple—your expected drive time, smart movers always build in extra time. A good rule of thumb is to multiply your normal travel time by 1.5 or even 2 during rush hours.

Space Out Multiple Trips

If you're doing multiple loads, don't plan them back-to-back during peak traffic windows. Try to schedule the second trip for late morning or early afternoon, once the roads clear a bit. This small change in timing can save you a lot of time and frustration.

Avoid Stress & Maximize Efficiency

Here’s the thing: if you can shift your move even half an hour, you’ll save time, stress, and potentially fuel/delays.

According to research, in large U.S. metro areas, delaying departure by 30 minutes outside the busiest commute window can save commuters up to 20–34 hours per year. Now, multiply that by heavy trucks and tight schedules in NJ, and a small schedule shift can make the move smoother and safer.

FAQs

What time is traffic the worst in New Jersey?

Traffic in New Jersey is typically worse during weekday rush hours, which are 6:30 am to 9:30 am and 3:30 pm to 6:30 pm. These periods experience the highest congestion on highways such as I-95, the Garden State Parkway, and Route 1.

Is it better to move on a weekday or a weekend in New Jersey?

Weekends are generally better for moving in New Jersey, especially Saturday mornings, when traffic is lighter. Weekdays often involve peak commute congestion, delivery delays, and stricter building access hours.

Can moving companies avoid rush hour traffic?

Yes, most professional moving companies plan their schedules to avoid rush hour. You can request an early morning or late morning slot to reduce delays and keep your move efficient.

How do weather conditions affect moving during rush hour in New Jersey?

Rain, snow, and fog can significantly slow down traffic and increase the risk of delays or accidents. Always check the forecast, especially in winter months, and consider rescheduling if conditions are unsafe.

Are there tolls to consider when planning a move in New Jersey?

Yes, New Jersey has multiple toll roads, including the NJ Turnpike, Garden State Parkway, and bridges into NYC and Philly. Be sure to factor tolls into your route planning and moving budget.
